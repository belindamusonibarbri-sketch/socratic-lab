How the Live Version Works

1
Student selects a scenario and reads the fact pattern, then types their initial legal argument or analysis into the chat.
2
Claude — operating under a locked Socratic system prompt — responds only with a probing question. It never confirms, corrects, or explains. It exposes gaps in reasoning by asking the student to justify each claim.
3
The student must respond to the question and defend their position. Claude continues probing until the student reaches a fully defended conclusion or exhausts a line of reasoning.
4
The student copies the full transcript using the button below the chat and submits it as their Canvas or D2L assignment. The instructor grades reasoning quality using the rubric below — not whether the student reached the "right" answer.
5
In the live version, the system prompt is invisible to students and cannot be overridden. Claude is constrained to ask one focused question per response and is explicitly instructed not to reward flattery or accept vague answers.
Reasoning Quality Indicators (for instructor rubric)
Identifies all relevant legal elements
Applies rule to specific facts
Anticipates counter-arguments
Responds without circular logic
Distinguishes analogous cases
Reaches defensible conclusion
